Cleaning Instructions

This topic provides instructions on how to clean your Basler camera.

Cleaning the Sensor

Avoid cleaning the surface of the camera’s sensor if possible. If you must clean it, follow these guidelines:

  • Before starting, disconnect the camera from power (and I/O power, if applicable).
  • Use a soft, lint-free cloth dampened with a small amount of window cleaner.
  • Because electrostatic discharge can damage the sensor, you must use a cloth that won’t generate static during cleaning (cotton is a good choice).
  • Before reconnecting the camera to power, make sure that the window cleaner has evaporated.

Cleaning the Housing or the Camera Front

If your camera is delivered with a housing or a camera front and you want to clean the housing or the front, follow these guidelines:

  • Before starting, disconnect the camera from camera power (and I/O power, if applicable).
  • Use a soft, dry cloth. To remove severe stains, use s soft cloth dampened with a small quantity of detergent, then wipe dry.
  • Because electrostatic discharge can damage the camera, you must use a cloth that won’t generate static during cleaning (cotton is a good choice).
  • Do not use solvents or thinners. They can damage the surface finish.
  • Before reconnecting the camera to power, make sure that the detergent has evaporated.

Cleaning the IR Cut Filter

If your camera is equipped with an IR cut filter and you want to clean it, follow these guidelines:

  • Try not to touch the IR cut filter and do not clean the IR cut filter mechanically. The glass of the IR cut filter can break if you apply too much pressure.
  • Use clean, oil-free compressed air to clean the IR cut filter. Be careful not to apply too much air pressure.
